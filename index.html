Polycythemia Vera on Ruxolitinib

Dynamic prognostic model for patients with polycythemia vera treated with second-line ruxolitinib after hydroxyurea failure. Evaluates response parameters at 6 months to predict event-free survival and identify candidates for treatment optimization.

Score Variables — Assessed Over First 6 Months
Absence of spleen reduction ≥50% at both 3 and 6 months
No ≥50% reduction in palpable spleen length (SR50) from baseline at both month 3 and month 6. Highest-weighted variable in the model. Note: in patients without baseline splenomegaly who never develop a palpable spleen, SR50 is considered not applicable (criterion absent → 0 points).
HR 2.65 (95% CI 1.25–5.82) · p = 0.009
If SR50 absent at both timepoints → +2.5 points
+0 pts
Ruxolitinib <10 mg BID at ≥1 timepoint
Subtherapeutic ruxolitinib dosing (below the standard 10 mg twice daily) at one or more timepoints (baseline, 3 or 6 months). Most commonly driven by mild, transient anemia (Hb 10–12 g/dL); however, reductions outside label recommendations are associated with worse EFS. Patients underdosed at all timepoints carry a markedly higher risk.
HR 1.94 (95% CI 1.01–3.99) · p = 0.047
